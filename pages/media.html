Projects & Media Coverage

Two Platforms

SUTRA - Multilingual LLMs and Enterprise AI Platform

Developed multilingual LLM architecture that decouples concept learning from language learning. Treats languages like modalities, enabling new language addition without retraining the base concept model. Addresses gaps in existing LLMs that were primarily trained in English. [Introducing SUTRA] [SUTRA Tokenizer] [SUTRA OP]

Jio AI Cloud

Deployed AI-powered multilingual search and understanding at national scale on Jio AI Cloud serving 400M+ users across multimodal inputs (text, images, audio, video, documents) on CPU and edge infrastructure. Handled massive scale processing of petabytes (400M users, 5K average files/user) with custom text-image models and vector indexes. Generated $10M+ in ARR. [Jio AI Cloud]

ZAPPY AI

ZAPPY is a social AI companion app where you can chat (text, voice, video) with AI characters, create your own and use features like drafting messages and sharing moments. Zappy supports long-term memory, no blind spots and contextual conversations. More than 20M+ messages have been shared on ZAPPY so far from 100K+ users.

Auto Cameo

Service automating personalized video messages from celebrities to fans at scale. Partnership with Jio Platforms during 2023 IPL enabled cricket fans to receive personalized videos from stars like Rohit Sharma and Virat Kohli (~300k users). Uses TWO's HALO-2 engine for high-fidelity AI video generation. [Announcing SUTRA Avatar]

University of Colorado

Open World Recognition is a machine learning paradigm developed during my PhD to enable models to operate in real-world environments where new concepts appear continuously. Unlike traditional closed-set systems, Open World Recognition allows models to detect previously unseen concepts, incrementally learn them, and retain prior knowledge without catastrophic forgetting. [CVPR 2016: Towards Open Set Deep Networks] [CVPR 2015: Towards Open World Recognition] [CVPR 2016 Spotlight Talk] [CVPR 2015 Oral Presentation]

Massachusetts Institute of Technology

MIT Media Lab

VisionBlocks was an on-demand, in-browser computer vision application building platform for users without prior programming experience. Users could create and share computer vision applications using simple drag-and-drop blocks with Scratch Programming Language-like interface. VisionBlocks was awarded Google's App Engine Research award for innovative concept of bringing computer vision to the masses.

MIT McGovern Institute of Brain Research

Research in the DiCarlo Lab on pedestrian detection systems and benchmarking vision models inspired by the primate visual cortex (V1-like models).
