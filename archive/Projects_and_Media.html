SUTRA - Multilingual LLMs & Enterprise AI

During the launch of ZAPPY in APAC market, we noticed that existing LLMs (GPT 3.5, Aya, Okapi, BLOOM etc.) were failing in non-english languages. Most LLMs and tokenizers were mostly trained in English (see tokenizer comparison). SUTRA Multilingual architecture was designed to address this gap. It decoupled concept learning from language learning, inspired by human cognition (see: LeCun, Gibson), and treats languages like modalities, similarly to how multi-modal LLMs project audio/images to the LLMs embedding space. This enabled adding new languages without re-training the base concept model.

University of Colorado

Open World Recognition is a machine learning paradigm I developed during my PhD to enable models to operate in real-world environments where new concepts appear continuously. Unlike traditional closed-set systems that recognize only predefined categories, Open World Recognition allows models to detect previously unseen concepts, incrementally learn them over time, and retain prior knowledge without catastrophic forgetting. The framework combines open-set recognition (detecting unknowns) with incremental learning (adding new knowledge), making it essential for applications such as robotics, autonomous driving, and other dynamic real-world systems. The core ideas were introduced in a CVPR 2015 oral paper and later extended to deep learning models in a CVPR 2016 paper. Since then, Open World Recognition has become an active area of research across government and industry, including major investments such as the $40M+ SAIL-ON program funded by IARPA.
