Generating digital avatar (2023)
US Patent 11,544,886

This patent introduces a system and method for generating lifelike digital avatars capable of synthesizing human appearance, speech, expressions, and behaviors in real time from non-video inputs (such as text, audio, or expression signals). The invention enables controllable, semi-autonomous, or fully autonomous digital humans for applications in media, customer engagement, education, and immersive experiences

SLAM for AR/VR Applications

My patented research in event-based vision and SLAM (Simultaneous Localization and Mapping) advances the foundation of low-power, low-latency AR/VR systems. By integrating Dynamic Vision Sensors (DVS) with CMOS cameras and IMUs, these innovations address the core challenges of tracking, mapping, depth estimation, and relocalization in real-world, resource-constrained devices. This portfolio demonstrates leadership in next-generation AR/VR sensing and mapping technologies, shaping the future of untethered, mobile immersive platforms. Collectively, these patents establish a comprehensive event-driven SLAM pipeline — covering feature detection, tracking, depth estimation, mapping, loop closure, and relocalization — all optimized for low-power AR/VR platforms. They showcase my leadership in bridging novel sensing (DVS) with scalable AR/VR system design for real-world adoption.

Camera pose determination and tracking (2020)
US Patent 10,839,547

Proposed a monocular SLAM framework that merges asynchronous DVS events with keyframes from standard cameras. Achieves robust pose estimation and map generation under fast motion and challenging lighting, reducing drift and enhancing real-time AR/VR experiences.

Camera pose and plane estimation using active markers and a dynamic vision sensor (2020)
US Patent 10,529,074

Invented an event-driven feature detection method that processes asynchronous pixel-level DVS events for SLAM, detecting edges, corners, and motion at microsecond resolution. Enables robust, low-latency tracking under high-speed motion and HDR conditions where conventional vision fails.

Semantic mapping for low-power augmented reality using dynamic vision sensor (2023)
US Patent 11,558,551 and US Patent 10,812,711

Designed an event-based loop closure and relocalization framework that uses DVS feature descriptors for place recognition, drift correction, and pose recovery. Provides long-term stability and accuracy in extended AR/VR sessions while reducing compute and energy cost.

Semi-dense depth estimation from a dynamic vision sensor (DVS) stereo pair and a pulsed speckle pattern projector (2021)
US Patent 11,143,879

Created a depth estimation system that fuses DVS stereo pairs with a pulsed speckle projector, enabling depth recovery in low-light or low-texture scenes. Produces low-latency, semi-dense depth maps robust to static or dynamic environments, ideal for AR/VR and mobile vision.

CMOS-assisted inside-out dynamic vision sensor tracking for low power mobile platforms (2022)
US Patent 11,381,741 and US Patent 11,202,006

Advanced a hybrid tracking framework combining DVS, CMOS, and IMU data with a confidence-driven sensor scheduler. Delivers energy-efficient SLAM with semantic mapping and persistent AR overlays, powering next-gen mobile AR/VR devices.
